Materials and Craft Methods

  • Clay and Terracotta: hand-molded, kiln-fired ceramics and ritual forms.
  • Brass and Bronze: lost-wax casting for masks and plaques.
  • Wood (Ebony/Iroko): carved masks, stools, and symbolic figures.
  • Glass Beads: Krobo and Maasai beadwork.
  • Cotton, Raffia, Wool: kente, baskets, textiles.
  • Natural Dyes: indigo, mud, and bark patterns.

Photo: Ewe kente stripes, ZSM / Commons (CC BY-SA).

Why These Artifacts Matter

African artworks carry stories of identity, spirituality, lineage, trade, and celebration. ArtByAfricans highlights origin, craft style, and artisan details so buyers understand the cultural context behind every piece.
